About Us

Newport Village

Newport, historically known as Ballyveaghan (Irish: Baile Uí Fhiacháin), is a small picturesque town in the barony of Burrishoole, County Mayo, Ireland. It is located on the west coast of Ireland, along the shore of Clew Bay, north of Westport.

The N59 road passes through the town. The county town of Castlebar is approx 18 km east of Newport. The Black Oak River flows through the centre of the town and there are pleasant walking paths along its grassy banks.

Newport has many distinct features, the elegant being the Seven Arch viaduct dominating the townscape. It was built in 1892 to carry the Westport-Achill railway line.

Visit the famous stained glass windows in St.Patrick's Church.

Newport's distinctive Sandstone Viaduct.

Newport has a very striking disused railway viaduct crossing the river which, together with the Roman Catholic church on top of the hill, dominate the town and create a picturesque appearance.

St. Patrick's Catholic Church, built in 1914 in the Irish Romanesque Revival style by Rudolph M. Butler is so imposing that it is usually referred to as 'Newport Cathedral'. It has a magnificent stained glass east window of The Last Judgement, the last window completed by Harry Clarke in 1930. Burrishoole Friary and Gráinne O'Malley's Rockfleet Castle are both just to the west of the town.

The town is a popular angling and tourist centre with many local fishermen offering to bring visitors out fishing in Clew Bay.
